Future of Humanitarian Work
Come help us conceive the Future of Humanitarian work! We, at Quicksand as part of our Future Fiction program, are looking for collaborators to present a global perspective on the the topic of humanitarian crisis response.
Please reach out to us if you are interested in collaborating with us! hello@quicksand.co.in
However unpredictable the future may seem, many are involved with foreseeing and planning around the greatest challenges it holds. Be it water shortages, a frenzied climate, depleting resources or unstable governments, the world we live in is constantly facing complex humanitarian crises. As many are looking for ways to tackle the causes of these forces, join us in exploring how to mitigate their short-term impact on affected communities and vulnerable regions.
Together, let's consider, explore, unearth the future needs of humanitarian crises and how aid communities will come together to face these odds.
The work will be presented at the Barbican in April 2019 as part of The Future of Humanitarian Work pop-up exhibit. By curating such diverse perspectives we will present an adventurous dive into the emergencies of tomorrow through the lived experiences of its humanitarian workers. Disasters, their preparedness as well their mitigation might undergo far more transformation than visible now.
When new technologies, social data and human error undergo further mutation in the coming decades, the typology of humanitarian contexts, actors and response will transform. The exhibit puts on a playful garb while presenting a powerful transmedia experience that brings to life some of these future experiences.
We have started work with our partners NID, STBY, HLA, and Tandem Research.
